Racket Grip

When holding the racket, you want to be in continental grip. To find continental grip, look at the butt end of the racket while the strings are perpendicular to the ground. The racket handle will be in the shape of an octagon. Each side is a bevel, and consider the top bevel 1, and numerically increase going clockwise to find the rest of the bevel numbers. To get to continental grip, line up your right index knuckle with bevel 2 if you are right handed or left index knuckle on bevel 7 if you are left handed. Additionally, make sure your hand is at the very bottom of the racket.

Another way you may find easier to get the grip is to hold the racket under your non dominate armpit with the strings against your side. From there reach across and line your index knuckle with furthest topmost bevel.

A common mistake is to start at bevel 3 because that is the longest side. You must make sure that if you follow bevel 1, it will wrap all the way around the frame of the racket until you end up at bevel 5.
